Explain the difference between the DIV and SPAN tags. Provide a scenario where each is more appropriate to use.

DIV

  1. It's a block-level element used to group and structure content on a web page.
  2. It's commonly used to create divisions or sections of content, and it often contains other block-level and inline elements.
  3. It's commonly used for applying styles or layout structures, and it helps in organizing the content for styling and positioning purposes.

SPAN

  1. It's an inline-level element used to apply styles or manipulate a specific portion of text within a block-level element.
  2. It is often used when you want to apply styles, scripting, or other modifications to a small piece of text or content within a larger container.
  3. Unlike the div, which is a block-level element, the span is an inline element and is generally used for smaller, inline-level content.
